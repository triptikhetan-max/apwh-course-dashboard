  **I:** "The intended audience was British military and political readers who needed to understand the strategic implications of Indian railroads. This matters because Gardiner frames nationalist sentiment not as a moral or political issue but as a strategic concern — the language of a military officer assessing a potential threat."
  **P:** "The purpose was to assess the unintended political consequences of railroad construction in India for British strategic planning. This matters because Gardiner was not celebrating Indian unity — he was warning that it could become a problem for British control."
  **POV:** "As a British army officer, Gardiner's perspective was shaped by his military and imperial worldview. This explains why he describes growing Indian unity as a strategic concern rather than a positive development — he sees Indian nationalism through the lens of imperial security, not Indian self-determination."

### ITEM 7: Essay — SAQ Using 2 Documents

- **Content Type:** frq_writing (essay)
- **Title:** "Short Answer: HIPP on 2 Documents + Historical Argument"
- **Source:** Use Documents from Items 4 and 6 (2023 S1 Doc 5 + 2023 S2 Doc 2)
- **Scrape Instructions:** None — students already have these documents.
- **Content:**

**Prompt:**
"Using Document 3 (Han Chinese nationalist manifesto) and Document 5 (Egyptian woman's essay on economic equality), write a short answer (5-7 sentences) that:

1. Performs HIPP analysis on BOTH documents (at least 1 element per document)
2. Makes a comparative historical argument: Both documents represent challenges to established authority. How do the authors' different identities (ethnic nationalist vs. religious feminist) shape HOW they challenge authority?

This is harder than Module 6's essay because you are comparing two documents. Your HIPP analysis should drive the comparison."

- **Autograder Rubric:**
  - **Score 0:** Summary without HIPP, or HIPP without comparison.
  - **Score 1:** HIPP on at least 1 document + attempted comparison.
  - **Score 2:** HIPP on both documents + coherent comparative argument showing how identity shapes the nature of each challenge to authority.

- **Model Answer (H5P Reveal):**
> "Both the Han Chinese nationalist manifesto and the Egyptian woman's essay challenge established authority, but they do so from fundamentally different identity positions that shape their approaches. The nationalist manifesto, written by Han Chinese revolutionaries (POV), frames the Qing Dynasty as a foreign occupying force and calls for its complete overthrow — the authors' ethnic identity as Han Chinese made them see Manchu rule as illegitimate, which meant reform was insufficient; only revolution would do. The Egyptian essay, written by a Muslim woman feminist (POV), challenges social norms from within the Islamic tradition rather than rejecting it — her dual identity as both a Muslim and a feminist required her to show that Islam supports women's equality, making her approach reformist rather than revolutionary. The contrast reveals that how an author challenges authority depends on their relationship to the system: outsiders (like the Han nationalists vis-a-vis Manchu rule) seek overthrow, while insiders (like the Muslim feminist vis-a-vis Islamic tradition) seek reinterpretation."

- **Feedback if Score 0-1:** "Try to explicitly compare how the two authors' identities shape their different approaches to challenging authority."
- **Feedback if Score 2:** "Excellent comparative analysis! Using HIPP to drive a comparison between documents is exactly what the DBQ requires."

- **Order:** 7
