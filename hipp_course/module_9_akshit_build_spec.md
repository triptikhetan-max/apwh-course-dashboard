### ITEM 2: Article — "Pre-Write Strategy: 15 Minutes That Save Your Essay"

- **Content Type:** article
- **Title:** "Pre-Write Strategy: How to Use the First 15 Minutes"
- **Source:** GENERATE
- **Scrape Instructions:** None.
- **Content:**

*"On the real AP exam, you have 60 minutes for the DBQ: a recommended 15-minute reading/planning period plus 45 minutes to write. Most students waste the planning period by reading documents too slowly and not organizing their thoughts. Here is a better approach."*

**The 15-Minute Pre-Write Plan:**

**Minutes 1-2: Read the prompt. Twice.**
Underline the key words. What is the prompt actually asking? "Evaluate the extent to which" means you need to make an argument about degree — not just list facts.

**Minutes 3-10: Read all 7 documents. Source line FIRST.**
For each document:
1. Read the source line (author, date, type, context)
2. Skim the document content (30-45 seconds per doc)
3. In the margin, write the strongest HIPP element: H, I, P, or POV
4. Write a 3-word summary of what the doc shows

**Minutes 10-13: Group documents into 2-3 buckets.**
Look at your 3-word summaries. What themes emerge? Group the documents by argument, not by chronology. Each bucket becomes a body paragraph.

**Minutes 13-15: Draft your thesis.**
Your thesis should respond to the prompt, make an argument about "extent," and preview your buckets. Write it in the margin. You can refine it when you start writing.

**The Essay Structure:**

1. **Introduction** (3-4 sentences): Contextualization + Thesis
2. **Body Paragraph 1** (6-8 sentences): Topic sentence + 2-3 docs + HIPP on at least 1
3. **Body Paragraph 2** (6-8 sentences): Topic sentence + 2-3 docs + HIPP on at least 1
4. **Body Paragraph 3** (4-6 sentences): Topic sentence + 1-2 docs + HIPP on at least 1
5. **(Optional) Conclusion** (1-2 sentences): Only if time permits

**Scoring Targets:**

| Rubric Point | What You Need | Your Target |
|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| Thesis (1) | Historically defensible claim + line of reasoning | Write it in the first paragraph |
| Contextualization (1) | Broader historical context for the essay topic | 2-3 sentences in the introduction |
| Evidence — Description (1) | Accurately describe 3+ documents | Use docs as evidence in body paragraphs |
| Evidence — Support (1) | Use 6+ documents to support argument | Use all 7 if possible |
| Evidence — Outside (1) | 1 piece of evidence not in the documents | Add one outside example per body paragraph |
| Sourcing (1) | HIPP on 2+ documents, explaining relevance | Source at least 2 (aim for 4) |
| Complexity (1) | Multiple pathways — easiest: HIPP 4+ docs | If you source 4+ docs, you can earn this |

- **Autograder Rubric (7 points total — mirrors AP DBQ rubric):**

  **Thesis (0-1):**
  - **Score 0:** Restates prompt, is too vague, or makes no argument about "extent."
  - **Score 1:** Makes a historically defensible claim about the extent to which communist rule transformed Soviet/Chinese societies AND establishes a line of reasoning (previews supporting arguments).
  - **Grading keywords:** Must take a position on "extent" (significant/limited/mixed). Must mention specific types of transformation (education, gender, political control, economic policy, etc.).

  **Contextualization (0-1):**
  - **Score 0:** No context, or context that is too vague ("Communism existed in the 20th century").
  - **Score 1:** Provides broader historical context relevant to the prompt — e.g., Bolshevik Revolution, Marxist ideology, pre-communist conditions in Russia/China, global Cold War context. Must be more than a single sentence and must go beyond what the documents directly describe.

  **Evidence — Description (0-1):**
  - **Score 0:** Uses 0-2 documents.
  - **Score 1:** Accurately describes the content of 3+ documents.

  **Evidence — Support (0-1):**
  - **Score 0:** Documents listed but not connected to argument.
  - **Score 1:** Uses at least 6 documents to SUPPORT the argument (documents are connected to topic sentences and thesis, not just listed).

  **Evidence — Outside (0-1):**
  - **Score 0:** No evidence beyond the documents.
  - **Score 1:** At least 1 piece of specific, relevant historical evidence not found in the documents (e.g., specific policy names like the New Economic Policy, specific events like the Cultural Revolution, specific figures like Khrushchev's Secret Speech).

  **Sourcing (0-1):**
  - **Score 0:** No HIPP analysis that explains relevance.
  - **Score 1:** HIPP analysis on at least 2 documents that explains HOW or WHY the HIPP element is relevant to the argument.

  **Complexity (0-1):**
  - **Score 0:** Does not demonstrate complex understanding.
  - **Score 1:** Demonstrates complex understanding through one or more of: (a) HIPP analysis on 4+ documents, (b) explaining nuance (e.g., communism both transformed and preserved aspects of society), (c) making connections across time periods/regions, (d) qualifying/modifying the argument.
