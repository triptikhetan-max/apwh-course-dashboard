### ITEM 2: Article — "How to Weave HIPP into Body Paragraphs"

- **Content Type:** article
- **Title:** "The Bucket Method: How to Group Docs and Weave in HIPP"
- **Source:** GENERATE
- **Scrape Instructions:** None.
- **Content:**

**The Problem: HIPP as Afterthought**

Most students who learn HIPP treat it like a chore — a separate thing they bolt on after writing their essay. They write their body paragraphs, then awkwardly insert a sentence like "Also, the historical situation was..." at the end.

That does not work. Sourcing that sits separately from your argument looks like an afterthought — because it is one. The best DBQ essays integrate sourcing INTO the argument so smoothly that the reader barely notices it is there.

**The Bucket Method**

Here is how to do it. Before you write anything, organize your documents into "buckets" — thematic groups that will become your body paragraphs.

**Step 1: Read all documents and sort them into 2-3 groups by theme.**

Example prompt: "Evaluate the extent to which foreign involvement led to the collapse of the Qing Empire."

- **Bucket 1 (Foreign economic pressure):** Documents about Opium Wars, treaty ports, foreign investment
- **Bucket 2 (Internal instability made worse by foreign pressure):** Documents about peasant revolts, rural poverty, Taiping Rebellion
- **Bucket 3 (Revolutionary movements inspired by foreign ideas):** Documents about nationalism, Western liberal influence, anti-Manchu sentiment

**Step 2: For each document, decide which HIPP element is strongest and write 1-2 sentences of sourcing.**

You do not need all 4 elements for every document. Pick the strongest one or two. Quality over quantity.

**Step 3: Weave the sourcing INTO the paragraph.**

Here is the pattern for a body paragraph with sourcing:

1. **Topic sentence** (your argument for this paragraph)
2. **Document evidence** (what the document says/shows)
3. **HIPP sourcing** (1-2 sentences: why the document's context/purpose/audience/POV matters to your argument)
4. **Connection back to thesis** (how this supports your overall argument)

**Example paragraph structure:**

> *Foreign economic pressure weakened the Qing Dynasty by draining Chinese resources and undermining state sovereignty.* [Topic sentence] *Document 2 shows that British opium trade forced silver out of China and weakened Qing economic stability.* [Evidence] *As a radical European political theorist, Marx's perspective shaped his analysis of these events — he emphasized British economic aggression because it supported his broader argument about capitalism's destructive power, which means we should read this document as an ideological analysis, not a neutral historical account (POV). Nevertheless, Marx's core claim — that the opium trade weakened the Qing — is corroborated by other documents in the set.* [HIPP sourcing] *This pattern of foreign economic extraction illustrates how external pressure accelerated the decline of Qing authority.* [Connection to thesis]

**The 2-4-1 Rule**

For the sourcing point: source at least 2 documents.
For the complexity point: source at least 4 documents.
Each sourcing attempt: 1-2 sentences max.

Do not write a paragraph of sourcing for each document. You do not have time, and it derails your argument. Keep sourcing concise and purposeful.

### ITEM 3: Mini-DBQ #1 — 3 Documents (HIPP + Thesis + 1 Body Paragraph)

- **Content Type:** frq_writing (multi-part essay)
- **Title:** "Mini-DBQ Practice #1: 3 Documents"
- **Source:** 2023 Set 1 FRQ PDF — Documents 1, 3, 7 (Collapse of the Qing Empire)
- **Scrape Instructions:**
  1. Open `/Users/tripti/Downloads/final_pdfs/qc_pipeline/reports/hipp_research/released_ap_docs/frq_sets/2023_set1_frq.pdf`
  2. Navigate to the DBQ section.
  3. Extract **Documents 1, 3, and 7** — full source lines + full document text for each.
  4. Also extract the **DBQ prompt** verbatim.
  5. Preserve all original formatting.

  **Documents to extract:**
  - **Doc 1:** Local leaders/village elders petitioning for military intervention, c. 1846-1860s
  - **Doc 3:** Legal/diplomatic document about foreigners and extraterritoriality
  - **Doc 7:** Qing court abdication statement/proclamation, 1912
- **Content:**

**DBQ Prompt displayed to student:**
> "Evaluate the extent to which foreign involvement led to the collapse of the Qing Empire."

**3 Documents displayed:**
Show Documents 1, 3, and 7 from the 2023 Set 1 DBQ in styled document cards.

**Instructions:**

**Part A — HIPP Each Document (3 text areas)**
"For each document, write 1-2 sentences of HIPP analysis. You do not need all 4 elements — pick the strongest element for each document."

- Document 1 HIPP: ___
- Document 3 HIPP: ___
- Document 7 HIPP: ___

**Part B — Write a Thesis (1 text area)**
"Write a thesis statement that responds to the prompt. Your thesis should make an argument, not just restate the question."

**Part C — Write 1 Body Paragraph (1 text area)**
"Write ONE body paragraph that:
- Uses at least 2 of the 3 documents as evidence
- Weaves HIPP sourcing into the paragraph (at least 1 document must have sourcing)
- Supports your thesis"

- **Autograder Rubric:**
  - **Thesis (0-1):**
    - **Score 0:** Restates the prompt, is too vague, or does not make an argument.
    - **Score 1:** Makes a historically defensible claim that responds to the prompt and establishes a line of reasoning.

  - **Evidence (0-2):**
    - **Score 0:** No documents cited or documents used incorrectly.
    - **Score 1:** Uses 1 document correctly as evidence.
    - **Score 2:** Uses 2+ documents correctly as evidence.

  - **Sourcing (0-1):**
    - **Score 0:** No HIPP analysis, or HIPP that does not explain relevance.
    - **Score 1:** At least 1 document has HIPP analysis that explains how/why a HIPP element is relevant to the argument.

- **Model Answer (H5P Reveal):**

  **Thesis:** "Foreign involvement was the primary catalyst for the collapse of the Qing Empire, as external military pressure, extraterritorial privileges, and the ideological influence of Western political ideas combined to undermine Qing sovereignty and legitimacy from multiple directions simultaneously."

  **Body Paragraph:** "Foreign interference in Chinese legal sovereignty was a key factor in the collapse of the Qing Empire. Document 3 shows that foreign powers in China enjoyed extraterritorial privileges that allowed their citizens to operate outside Chinese law — a direct challenge to Qing authority within its own borders. The purpose of establishing extraterritoriality was not just legal convenience but a deliberate assertion of Western superiority over Chinese governance, which explains why these privileges became a rallying point for anti-Qing revolutionaries who argued that the dynasty was too weak to protect Chinese sovereignty (Purpose). Document 7, the Qing court's abdication statement of 1912, reveals that by the end, the dynasty itself acknowledged its inability to maintain order — written during the Xinhai Revolution, the abdication statement's deferential tone and emphasis on avoiding further bloodshed reflects a government that had lost the mandate of heaven and the military capacity to resist (Historical Situation). Together, these documents show that foreign involvement did not just weaken the Qing externally — it delegitimized the dynasty in the eyes of its own people."

- **Feedback:** Separate scores for thesis, evidence, and sourcing with targeted guidance.
- **Order:** 3

### ITEM 4: Mini-DBQ #2 — 4 Documents (HIPP + Thesis + 2 Body Paragraphs)

- **Content Type:** frq_writing (multi-part essay)

- **Source:** 2024 Set 2 FRQ PDF — Documents 1, 3, 4, 5 (Japanese Imperialism)
- **Scrape Instructions:**

  4. Also extract the **DBQ prompt** verbatim.
  5. Preserve all original formatting.

  **Documents to extract:**
  - **Doc 1:** Japanese official source supporting imperial expansion (early 20th c.)
  - **Doc 3:** Source critical of Japanese expansion
  - **Doc 4:** Korean migrant association in the US (non-Japanese perspective)
  - **Doc 5:** Japanese military/government analysis arguing seizing Northern China won't bring expected benefits
- **Content:**

**DBQ Prompt displayed to student:**
> "Evaluate the extent to which economic motives were the leading cause of Japanese imperialism in the period circa 1900-1945."

**4 Documents displayed:**
Show Documents 1, 3, 4, and 5 from the 2024 Set 2 DBQ in styled document cards.

**Instructions:**

**Part A — HIPP Each Document (4 text areas)**
"For each document, write 1-2 sentences of HIPP analysis. Pick the strongest element."

**Part B — Write a Thesis (1 text area)**
"Write a thesis statement responding to the prompt."

**Part C — Write 2 Body Paragraphs (2 text areas)**
"Write TWO body paragraphs. Each paragraph should:
- Use at least 1 document as evidence
- Weave HIPP sourcing into the paragraph
- Support your thesis
- The two paragraphs should make different but complementary arguments"

- **Autograder Rubric:**
  - **Thesis (0-1):** Same as Mini-DBQ #1.

  - **Evidence (0-2):**
    - **Score 0:** No documents cited or used incorrectly.
    - **Score 1:** Uses 1-2 documents correctly.
    - **Score 2:** Uses 3+ documents correctly across both paragraphs.

  - **Sourcing (0-1):**
    - **Score 0:** No HIPP analysis that explains relevance.
    - **Score 1:** At least 2 documents have HIPP analysis that explains how/why a HIPP element is relevant.

- **Model Answer (H5P Reveal):**

  **Thesis:** "While economic motives played a significant role in Japanese imperialism, strategic and ideological factors — including nationalist ambitions, military competition with Western powers, and Pan-Asian rhetoric — were equally if not more important in driving Japanese expansion between 1900 and 1945."

  **Body Paragraph 1:** "Economic motives were clearly present in Japanese imperial policy, but some Japanese officials themselves questioned whether economic gains would materialize. Document 5, a Japanese military analysis, argues that seizing direct control over Northern China would not bring the expected economic benefits — a remarkable admission from within the imperial establishment. As a pragmatic military critic writing for internal government consumption (POV), this author's skepticism about economic returns suggests that even within the Japanese government, the economic rationale for expansion was debatable. Document 1, by contrast, supports expansion — but as an official government/military source, its purpose was to justify expansion to domestic and international audiences, meaning its emphasis on economic benefits may reflect propaganda goals rather than genuine economic analysis (Purpose). Together, these documents suggest that the economic case for imperialism was as much a justification for other motives as it was a genuine driver."

  **Body Paragraph 2:** "Non-Japanese perspectives reveal the human costs that economic justifications obscured. Document 4, from a Korean migrant association in the United States, critiques Japanese colonization from the perspective of colonized people living in diaspora. The intended audience — the American public and potentially US policymakers — shaped the document's emphasis on Korean suffering and Japanese cruelty, framing the issue in terms that would appeal to American democratic values (Intended Audience). Document 3, critical of Japanese expansion for specific reasons, further challenges the economic narrative by highlighting the destructive consequences of imperialism that economic rationales conveniently ignored. These external perspectives show that reducing Japanese imperialism to economic motives obscures the military aggression, cultural domination, and political repression that characterized Japanese colonial rule."

- **Feedback:** Separate scores for thesis, evidence, and sourcing.
- **Order:** 4
