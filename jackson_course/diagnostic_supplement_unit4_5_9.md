## Unit 9: Globalization (c. 1900-present) -- 6 Questions

---

### Question S1 -- Topic 9.1: Advances in Technology and Exchange After 1900

**Stimulus:**

> "Between 1950 and 2010, the cost of a three-minute telephone call from New York to London fell from approximately $50 to less than $0.25 in inflation-adjusted dollars. During the same period, the cost of shipping a standard container of goods across the Atlantic declined by more than 90 percent. By 2015, more than three billion people worldwide had access to the internet."
>
> -- Adapted from a 21st-century analysis of global communications trends

**Question:** The developments described in the passage most directly illustrate which of the following broader historical processes?

(A) The growth of nationalist movements that resisted Western cultural influence

(B) The reduction of geographic barriers to exchange through advances in communication and transportation technology

(C) The establishment of international organizations designed to regulate global trade

(D) The spread of democratic governance to formerly authoritarian states

**Correct Answer: (B)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 9.1 -- Advances in Technology and Exchange After 1900
KC-6.1.I.A: New modes of communication and transportation reduced the problem of geographic distance.

### Question S2 -- Topic 9.2: Technological Advances and Limitations After 1900: Disease

**Stimulus:**

> "In 1918, a devastating influenza pandemic swept across the globe, killing an estimated 50 to 100 million people worldwide -- more than the total military casualties of the First World War. The virus spread rapidly along shipping routes and railway lines, carried by soldiers returning home from the front. Nearly a century later, the development of antiviral medications and global surveillance networks allowed health authorities to contain outbreaks of diseases such as SARS and H1N1 far more quickly."
>
> -- Adapted from a historical overview of pandemic response

**Question:** The contrast between the 1918 pandemic and the later disease outbreaks described in the passage best illustrates which of the following?

(A) The inability of international organizations to coordinate effective public health responses

(B) The role of military conflicts in spreading infectious disease across borders

(C) The significant effects that both diseases and medical developments had on global populations

(D) The decline of global trade networks in the late twentieth century

**Correct Answer: (C)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 9.2 -- Technological Advances and Limitations After 1900: Disease
KC-6.1.III: Diseases, as well as medical and scientific developments, had significant effects on populations around the world.

### Question S3 -- Topic 9.4: Economics in the Global Age

**Stimulus:**

> "Following the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991, dozens of formerly state-controlled economies underwent rapid transitions toward market-based systems. International financial institutions such as the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund encouraged governments to privatize state-owned industries, reduce tariffs, and open their markets to foreign investment. Regional trade agreements such as NAFTA and the European Union's single market further accelerated the integration of national economies."
>
> -- Adapted from a study of post-Cold War economic transitions

**Question:** The economic changes described in the passage most directly resulted from which of the following?

(A) The success of communist economic planning in Eastern Europe during the Cold War

(B) A trend toward free-market economic policies and economic liberalization accelerated by the end of the Cold War

(C) The desire of developing nations to protect domestic industries from foreign competition

(D) The rejection of international trade agreements by major industrialized nations

**Correct Answer: (B)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 9.4 -- Economics in the Global Age
KC-6.3.I.D: In a trend accelerated by the end of the Cold War, many governments encouraged free-market economic policies and promoted economic liberalization in the late 20th century.
KC-6.3.II.B: Multinational corporations and regional trade agreements reflected the spread of free-market economics throughout the world.

### Question S4 -- Topic 9.5: Calls for Reform and Responses After 1900

**Stimulus:**

> "In 1995, delegates from 189 countries gathered at the United Nations Fourth World Conference on Women in Beijing. The resulting Platform for Action called on governments to guarantee equal access to education, health care, and employment for women and girls. In the decades that followed, female enrollment in secondary education rose significantly across South Asia and sub-Saharan Africa, and women entered professions -- including law, medicine, and elected office -- that had previously been dominated by men in most societies."
>
> -- Adapted from a summary of global gender equity initiatives

**Question:** The developments described in the passage best support which of the following claims about the twentieth and twenty-first centuries?

(A) Gender hierarchies remained entirely unchanged despite international conferences and declarations.

(B) Rights-based discourses challenged old assumptions about gender, and participation in political and professional roles became more inclusive.

(C) International organizations were primarily focused on economic development rather than social reform.

(D) Access to education declined in the developing world as a result of globalization.

**Correct Answer: (B)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 9.5 -- Calls for Reform and Responses After 1900
KC-6.3.III.i: Rights-based discourses challenged old assumptions about race, class, gender, and religion.
KC-6.3.III.ii: Access to education and participation in new political and professional roles became more inclusive.

### Question S5 -- Topic 9.6: Globalized Culture After 1900

**Stimulus:**

> "By the early 2000s, a teenager in Lagos could watch the same Hollywood blockbuster on opening weekend as a teenager in Los Angeles, listen to Korean pop music on a streaming platform, and purchase clothing from a Swedish fast-fashion brand -- all while communicating with friends across three continents through social media. At the same time, Nigerian filmmakers produced over 1,500 movies annually, making 'Nollywood' the second-largest film industry in the world by volume."
>
> -- Adapted from a cultural analysis of globalization in the 21st century

**Question:** Which of the following best describes the historical process illustrated by the passage?

(A) The decline of local cultural production in response to Western economic dominance

(B) The restriction of consumer culture to wealthy, industrialized nations

(C) The globalization of popular and consumer culture, which increasingly transcended national borders while also stimulating local cultural production

(D) The replacement of traditional economic systems by centrally planned economies

**Correct Answer: (C)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 9.6 -- Globalized Culture After 1900
KC-6.3.IV.ii: Arts, entertainment, and popular culture increasingly reflected the influence of a globalized society.
KC-6.3.IV.iii: Consumer culture became globalized and transcended national borders.

### Question S6 -- Topic 9.7: Resistance to Globalization After 1900

**Stimulus:**

> "In 1999, approximately 40,000 protesters gathered in Seattle to demonstrate against the World Trade Organization's ministerial conference. Participants included labor union members who feared that free trade would drive down wages, environmentalists who argued that trade agreements prioritized corporate profits over ecological protection, and activists from developing nations who contended that globalization disproportionately benefited wealthy countries. Similar protests occurred at subsequent international economic summits throughout the early 2000s."
>
> -- Adapted from a historical account of the anti-globalization movement

**Question:** The protests described in the passage are best understood as an example of which of the following?

(A) Movements that protested the inequality of the environmental and economic consequences of global integration

(B) Nationalist revolutions aimed at establishing independent states

(C) Cold War-era ideological conflicts between capitalist and communist blocs

(D) Efforts by international organizations to expand free-trade agreements

**Correct Answer: (A)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 9.7 -- Resistance to Globalization After 1900
KC-6.3.II.C: Movements throughout the world protested the inequality of the environmental and economic consequences of global integration.
KC-6.3.IV.iv: Responses to rising cultural and economic globalization took a variety of forms.

## Unit 4: Transoceanic Interconnections (c. 1450-1750) -- 2 Questions

---

### Question S7 -- Topic 4.5: Maritime Empires Maintained and Developed

**Stimulus:**

> "The Dutch East India Company, established in 1602, was granted a monopoly by the Dutch government on all trade east of the Cape of Good Hope. The company was authorized to negotiate treaties, wage war, establish colonies, and mint its own currency. At its peak, the company employed tens of thousands of workers, operated a private army, and controlled a network of trading posts stretching from South Africa to Japan. Similar organizations, including the British East India Company, were established by rival European states."
>
> -- Adapted from a history of early modern commerce

**Question:** The type of organization described in the passage best illustrates which of the following developments in the period 1450-1750?

(A) The decline of long-distance trade networks as a result of political instability

(B) The use of joint-stock companies, influenced by mercantilist principles, by rulers and merchants to finance exploration and compete in global trade

(C) The rejection of mercantilist policies by European governments in favor of free trade

(D) The establishment of democratic institutions in European overseas colonies

**Correct Answer: (B)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 4.5 -- Maritime Empires Maintained and Developed
KC-4.1.IV.C: Joint-stock companies, influenced by mercantilist principles, were used by rulers and merchants to finance exploration and to compete against one another in global trade.

### Question S8 -- Topic 4.4: Maritime Empires Established

**Stimulus:**

> "In the early seventeenth century, the Tokugawa shogunate issued a series of edicts that severely restricted foreign trade. Japanese subjects were forbidden from traveling abroad, and most European merchants were expelled from the country. Only a small number of Dutch and Chinese traders were permitted to conduct limited commerce through the port of Nagasaki. These policies remained in effect for more than two centuries."
>
> -- Adapted from a study of early modern Japanese foreign policy

**Question:** The policies described in the passage are best understood as an example of which of the following?

(A) The expansion of European colonial control over Asian markets

(B) The adoption of restrictive or isolationist trade policies by some Asian states seeking to limit the disruptive effects of European-dominated long-distance trade

(C) The spread of Enlightenment ideas about free trade to East Asia

(D) The decline of Chinese commercial influence in the Pacific region

**Correct Answer: (B)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 4.4 -- Maritime Empires Established
KC-4.3.II.A.i: Some Asian states sought to limit the disruptive economic and cultural effects of European-dominated long-distance trade by adopting restrictive or isolationist trade policies.

### Question S9 -- Topic 5.2: Nationalism and Revolutions in the Period from 1750 to 1900

**Stimulus:**

> "Fellow citizens! The bonds that united us to Spain have been severed. We were left to ourselves, and from that abandonment came our opportunity. The veil has been torn asunder. We have seen the light, and we are no longer willing to remain in darkness. The chains have been broken; we have been freed, and our enemies seek to enslave us anew. For this reason, America fights with determination, and seldom has desperation been without avail."
>
> -- Adapted from a letter by Simon Bolivar, 1815

**Question:** The ideas expressed in the passage most directly reflect the influence of which of the following?

(A) Mercantilist economic theories that encouraged colonial self-sufficiency

(B) Enlightenment philosophies emphasizing natural rights and self-governance, as reflected in revolutionary documents

(C) Communist ideology calling for the abolition of private property

(D) Romantic nationalism focused on preserving indigenous cultural traditions

**Correct Answer: (B)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 5.2 -- Nationalism and Revolutions in the Period from 1750 to 1900
KC-5.3.I.B: The ideas of Enlightenment philosophers, as reflected in revolutionary documents -- including Bolivar's "Letter from Jamaica" -- influenced resistance to existing political authority, often in pursuit of independence and democratic ideals.

### Question S10 -- Topic 5.8: Reactions to the Industrial Economy from 1750 to 1900

**Stimulus:**

> "The history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles. Freeman and slave, patrician and plebeian, lord and serf, guild-master and journeyman, in a word, oppressor and oppressed, stood in constant opposition to one another. The modern bourgeois society that has sprouted from the ruins of feudal society has not done away with class antagonisms. It has but established new classes, new conditions of oppression."
>
> -- Adapted from a political pamphlet, 1848

**Question:** The ideas expressed in the passage most directly contributed to which of the following developments?

(A) The expansion of mercantilist trade policies by European colonial powers

(B) The development of ideologies such as socialism and communism as responses to industrial capitalism

(C) The establishment of constitutional monarchies across Western Europe

(D) The growth of nationalist movements that unified Germany and Italy

**Correct Answer: (B)**

**CED Mapping:** Topic 5.8 -- Reactions to the Industrial Economy from 1750 to 1900
KC-5.3.IV.A.ii: Discontent with established power structures encouraged the development of various ideologies, including those espoused by Karl Marx, and the ideas of socialism and communism.
