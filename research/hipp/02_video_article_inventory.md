## 12. Framework Comparison Table: HIPP vs. HAPP vs. HIPPO vs. HAPPY vs. SOAPSTone

### Side-by-Side Comparison

| Element | HIPP | HAPP | HIPPO | HAPPY | HAPP-E | SOAPSTone |

### Key Differences Explained

**HIPP vs. HAPP:** Functionally identical. HIPP uses "Intended audience" (I) while HAPP uses "Audience" (A). The difference is purely in the acronym letter, not the underlying skill. Some teachers prefer HIPP because it is more memorable; the College Board materials tend to use HAPP.

**HIPPO vs. HIPP:** HIPPO adds "O" for Outside Information/Evidence. This reminds students that the DBQ also requires evidence beyond the documents (a separate rubric point). HIPPO bundles two rubric points (sourcing + outside evidence) into one acronym.

**HAPPY vs. HIPP:** HAPPY adds "Y" for "Why is this relevant?" This prompts students to explain how the sourcing element connects to their argument -- which is actually required to earn the sourcing point. Some teachers argue HAPPY is superior because it reminds students to do the explanatory step that many forget.

**HAPP-E vs. HIPPO:** Same concept. HAPP-E uses "Evidence beyond documents" while HIPPO uses "Outside information." Both remind students of the separate outside evidence point.

**SOAPSTone vs. HIPP:** SOAPSTone is broader and originated from AP English. It adds Subject (what the document is about) and Tone (emotional/rhetorical quality), which go beyond what AP History DBQ sourcing requires. SOAPSTone is more useful for rhetorical analysis essays and AP English. Some AP World teachers use it early in the year before transitioning to HIPP for DBQ-specific work.

### Which Framework to Use?

| Situation | Recommended Framework | Rationale |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|
| Teaching DBQ sourcing for the first time | HIPP or HAPPY | Simple, memorable; HAPPY adds the crucial "why" step |
| Preparing for the AP exam | HIPP or HAPP | Matches what is strictly needed for the sourcing rubric point |
| Teaching both sourcing and outside evidence | HIPPO or HAPP-E | Bundles two rubric points into one acronym |
| Transitioning from AP English | SOAPSTone to HIPP | Start with familiar framework, then narrow to AP History needs |
| Advanced students aiming for complexity | HIPPO + extra sourcing | Complexity can be earned by sourcing 4+ documents |
