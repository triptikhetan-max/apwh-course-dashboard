# CED Data -- Topic 5.1: The Enlightenment

## Unit: 5 -- Revolutions
## Exam Weight: 12-15%
## Time Period: c. 1750-1900

## Thematic Focus
- Cultural Developments and Interactions (CDI)
- Social Interactions and Organization (SIO)

## Learning Objectives
- LO A: Explain the intellectual and ideological context in which revolutions swept the Atlantic world from 1750 to 1900.
- LO B: Explain how the Enlightenmentaffected societies over time.

## Essential Knowledge (Historical Developments)
- KC-5.3.I.A: Enlightenment philosophies applied new ways of understanding and empiricist approaches to both the natural world and human relationships; they also reexamined the role that religion playedinpubliclifeandemphasizedthe importance of reason. Philosophers developed new political ideas about the individual, natural rights, and the social contract.
- KC-5.3.II.i: Nationalism also became a major force shaping the historical development of states and empires.
- KC-5.3.IV.B: Demandsforwomen’ssuffrageandan emergent feminism challenged political and gender hierarchies.
